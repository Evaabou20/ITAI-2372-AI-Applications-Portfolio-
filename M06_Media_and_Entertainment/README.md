**M06: AI in Media and Entertainment**

This module explores the dual nature of AI in the creative industries: its role as a powerful tool for content production and the significant ethical and legal challenges it introduces. 

**Core Learning Objectives**
* **AI as a Creative Partner:** Understanding how algorithms assist in music composition (AIVA, Amper), art generation, and automated video editing.
* **Operational Efficiency:** Analyzing the shift from manual labor to AI workloads for tasks like closed captioning, transcription, and noise reduction.
* **Deepfakes & Misinformation:** Evaluating the technical mechanics and societal risks of highly realistic AI-generated synthetic media.
* **Industry Shift:** Examining the statistics of the sector, where social media now accounts for roughly 70% of the entertainment and media industry.

**Key Insights from "Entertainment and Media" Analysis**
* **Content Automation:** AI tools now handle what used to require entire teams, such as separating audio tracks and generating summaries, drastically reducing production costs.
  
**Ethical & Legal Barriers:**
* **Copyright & IP:** The ongoing debate regarding AI training on protected data without citation.
* **Job Displacement:** The reduction of roles for junior animators, editors, and transcriptionists.
* **Algorithmic Manipulation:** How platforms use AI to maximize engagement, often leading to addictive consumption patterns.
* **Harmful Representation:** The risk of AI reinforcing stereotypes if trained on biased datasets, impacting cultural discourse.
