**M09: Case Study - Facial Recognition in Law Enforcement**

This module features a comprehensive research paper analyzing the technical capabilities, ethical dilemmas, and societal impacts of facial recognition technology within policing.

**Case Study Overview**
* **The Technology:** Analysis of AI systems that map facial features (nodal points) to identify or verify individuals in real-time or from recorded footage.
* **Core Benefits:** Enhanced public safety, efficient suspect identification, and assistance in locating missing persons.
* **The Controversy:** A critical look at how "black box" algorithms can lead to life-altering consequences if not properly regulated.

**Critical Analysis: Ethics & Bias**

The paper explores the significant risks associated with deploying this AI without proper safeguards:
* **Demographic Disparities:** Discussion of the "Gender Shades" study and NIST findings, which highlight higher error rates for women and people of color.
* **Privacy & Surveillance:** The tension between collective security and the individual right to anonymity in public spaces.
* **Lack of Transparency:** The challenge of "proprietary algorithms" where law enforcement may not fully understand how a match was determined.
* **False Positives:** The real-world danger of misidentification leading to wrongful arrests.

**Proposed Solutions**
* Implementation of strict **Data Governance** policies.
* Requirement for **Human-in-the-loop** verification (AI as a tool, not the final judge).
* Calls for federal regulation and standardized accuracy testing across diverse populations.
