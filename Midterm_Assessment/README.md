**Midterm Assessment: Case Study on AI Abuse & Ethics**

This folder contains the mid-course evaluation and an in-depth case study analyzing the potential for **algorithmic abuse** and ethical challenges within the media industry. 

**Featured Project: The "Dark Side" of Personalization (Netflix Case Study)**

This research paper investigates how recommendation systems, while designed for convenience, can lead to systemic issues when misused or left unchecked. 
* **Algorithmic Bias & Abuse:** Analysis of how "Filter Bubbles" can isolate users, limiting their exposure to diverse viewpoints and creating a narrow digital reality.
* **Data Exploitation:** Examining the fine line between "personalization" and the invasive tracking of user behavior (watch duration, search history, and even thumbnail clicks).
* **Societal Impact:** How prioritizing engagement over artistic diversity can lead to the "echo chamber" effect, a significant ethical concern in modern AI applications.
* **Accountability:** A discussion on the need for **Explainable AI (XAI)** to prevent "Black Box" algorithms from making opaque decisions that influence public discourse.

**Core Midterm Competencies**

This assessment also valudated a broader understanding of AI ethics and foundations:
* **The Turing Test & Searle's Chinese Room:** Philosophical debates on whether AI truly "understands" the data it manipulates.
* **Global AI Regulation:** Comparative study of the **EU AI Act** and its focus on preventing high-risk AI abuses.
* **Generative AI Risks:** Analyzing the potential for deepfakes and misinformation in the current AI landscape.
