**M01: Philosophy and AI**

This module explores the historical and philosophical foundations of Artificial Intelligence.

**Core Topics & Learning Objectives**
* **Philosophical Foundations:** Examining how Ontology (nature of existence), Epistemology (nature of knowledge), and Ethics (moral implications) serve as the base for AI.
* **Consciousness and Intelligence:** Exploring the **Turing Test** as a measure of intelligent behavior and the difference between simulating intelligence versus possessing it.
* **The Chinese Room Argument:** Analyzing John Searle’s thought experiment regarding whether machines can truly "understand" or are simply manipulating symbols.
* **Ethical Frameworks:** Identifying sources of bias in historical data and developer design choices to ensure algorithmic fairness.
* **Societal Impact:** Evaluating AI's role in reshaping human identity (digital twins) and the future of work, including job displacement and the rise of new industries.
* **Governance:** Balancing innovation with international regulation, such as the implications of **GDPR** on AI development.
